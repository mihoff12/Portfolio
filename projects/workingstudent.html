Digital Twin Project

The Digital Twin project focused on the virtual replication of a factory floor with its machines and robots. The particular challenge was to optimize complex 3D scans for Unity while maintaining visual quality.

Technical Focus: Inverse Kinematics

A special emphasis was placed on developing an efficient animation system for complex robotic arms. By utilizing Inverse Kinematics, I implemented a system that only requires the position data of the end effector to simulate realistic movements of the entire robotic arm. This significantly reduced the amount of data needed and enabled precise 1:1 simulation of the real equipment.

Distributed Rendering

Object-based Rendering

Distribution of rendering load by dividing different 3D objects across multiple devices. Each device renders a portion of the scene, and the results are combined.

Viewport Rendering

Division of the screen view into multiple areas rendered by different devices. The partial views are then composited into a complete image.

Tile-based Rendering

Subdivision of the screen into smaller tiles that can be dynamically distributed across various rendering nodes. This enables flexible load balancing based on the complexity of each tile.

For simulating these rendering approaches without physical device networks, I employed Docker containers. These functioned as virtual rendering nodes and allowed for the validation of concepts in a controlled environment.
