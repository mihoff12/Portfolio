Master Project

Project Overview

  • Engine: Unity
  • Programming Language: C#
  • Project Type: Academic Team Project (Team of 5)
  • Status: Completed

Project Description

As part of my Master's studies, I collaborated with a team of five students on a project titled "Mixed Reality Application with AI-Controlled Characters." We developed a VR Dungeon Fighter game where the player navigates through a prison-like dungeon, fights enemies, and faces a final boss. A unique feature is an AI-controlled dragon companion that assists the player throughout the journey.

Game Mechanics

While the complete game featured an extensive combat system, enemy AI, and boss fight mechanics, I personally mainly worked on the following aspects of the game:

VR Dungeon Exploration

The player explores a procedurally generated dungeon using a VR headset and moves through the environment with a "treadmill" platform that translates physical walking movements into in-game navigation.

AI Companion System

An AI-controlled dragon accompanies the player, providing combat assistance, healing, and guidance through the dungeon when a room is cleared of enemies.

Other team members developed complementary game mechanics such as:

  • Combat System: Melee combat mechanics and player interaction with enemies
  • Enemy AI: Behavior patterns for different types of enemies with varying attack strategies
  • Boss Fight Mechanics: Complex AI system for the final boss with multiple attack phases
  • VR Integration: Implementation of the VR controls and treadmill platform connectivity

My Role & Responsibilities

In this team project, I was primarily responsible for two core components:

  • AI Dragon Companion: Developed the complete logic and behavior of the companion, including steering behaviors, finite state machine (FSM), and various action modes
  • Dungeon Generation: Implementation of a procedurally generated dungeon using the Depth-First Search algorithm
  • 3D Modeling: Creation of the dragon model, dungeon rooms, and interior elements
  • Animation & Effects: Animation of the dragon and implementation of particle effects for various gameplay elements

Technical Details: Dragon Companion

The AI dragon was fully implemented using steering behaviors and a hierarchical finite state machine:

  • Steering Behaviors:
    • Seek: The dragon flies toward the player
    • Arrive: The dragon slows down when close enough to the player
    • Obstacle Avoidance: The dragon avoids obstacles using raycasts
  • Hierarchical FSM:
    • Main states: "Support" and "Attack"
    • Support sub-states: "Follow", "Heal", "Guide"
    • Attack sub-states: "Damage", "Slow"

Dungeon Generation

For the procedurally generated game world, I developed a custom dungeon generator:

  • Based on the Depth-First Search algorithm
  • Placement of rooms in a grid with random orientation
  • Automatic generation of connecting corridors between rooms
  • Placement of the boss room at the end of the generated path

Intelligent Behavior of the Dragon Companion

The dragon adapts its behavior dynamically to the game situation:

  • Switches to "Support" mode when no enemies are nearby
  • Heals the player when their health falls below a threshold
  • Guides the player to the next room by marking torches
  • Attacks the nearest enemy when foes are within attack range
  • Slows down enemies when the player is in distress
